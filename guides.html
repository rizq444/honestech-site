Brake Service 101 — simple guide

Brake Pads

  • Ceramic
  • Semi-Metallic
  • Organic

Ceramic: Quiet, low dust. Great for daily driving.
Semi-metallic: Stronger bite, may be noisier; good for heavier use.
Organic: Softer, less durable; budget option.

Brake Rotors

  • Solid / Vented
  • Drilled
  • Slotted

Vented: Most common; helps cooling.
Drilled/Slotted: Can help gas/dust removal; not necessary for most daily cars.
Choose the rotor type your vehicle is designed for.

Hardware (shims & clips)

New hardware helps pads slide smoothly and reduce noise. A good kit includes shims, spring clips, and sometimes new pins/boots. Always replace worn hardware with the new pads.

Filters & Tune-Up 101

Engine Air Filter

Keeps dust out of the engine. Check every 12k–15k miles (sooner in dusty areas). A clean filter improves throttle response and protects the engine.

Cabin Air Filter

Cleans the air you breathe inside the car. Replace about yearly or if you notice smells/weak airflow. Helps defog windows faster.

Oil Filters

Spin-on: Self-contained metal canister.
Cartridge: Paper element in a reusable housing (bring a new O-ring).

Spark Plugs

Copper: ~30k mi.   Platinum: ~60k mi.   Iridium: ~90k–120k mi. Use what your car spec calls for.
